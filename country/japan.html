About live cams in Japan

Japan has arguably the densest public-webcam culture in the world. Municipalities, rail operators, hotels and volunteers keep official 24/7 cameras on river levels, level crossings, coastlines and Mt. Fuji from a dozen angles — a legacy of using live cameras for disaster awareness that doubles as some of the most reliable scenery streaming anywhere.

Much of this collection comes from dedicated operators — 草津町公式YouTubeチャンネル (18 feeds), 朝日新聞LIVE (14 feeds), and sozoro walk (14 feeds) — channels that keep cameras running year-round rather than posting one-off clips.

Resolution figures here are measured, not scraped from titles: Camlisted records the real playback quality when a feed is opened. 123 of these feeds have been measured so far — 26 in 4K, 10 in QHD, 64 in full HD, and 19 in HD.

Condition tags mark what a clip actually shows, so you can jump straight to a scenario: currently 25 night scenes, 5 rain scenes, 2 snow scenes, and 7 accident scenes tagged in Japan.

The longest-running live camera in this set has been broadcasting continuously since 2020 — “いわて花巻空港ライブ映像”.
