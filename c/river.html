About river / lake cams

River cams are slow television in the best sense — barge traffic, changing water levels, birds working the banks. In flood season they turn practical fast: locals genuinely use these feeds to watch levels rise hour by hour.

Much of this collection comes from dedicated operators — Projeto Vale Vivo (5 feeds), ぐんまの河川ライブカメラ(東部地域) (5 feeds), and 小清水町役場ライブカメラ (4 feeds) — channels that keep cameras running year-round rather than posting one-off clips.

Resolution figures here are measured, not scraped from titles: Camlisted records the real playback quality when a feed is opened. 32 of these feeds have been measured so far — 3 in 4K, 2 in QHD, 15 in full HD, and 9 in HD.

The longest-running live camera in this set has been broadcasting continuously since 2019 — “MKE River Cam - Milwaukee, Wisconsin - by MKE.com”.

Frequently asked questions

How many river / lake live cams are there?

101 live river / lake cameras are streaming right now, plus 12 recorded clips, with the deepest coverage in Japan, United States, Russia, and Brazil. The list is re-verified every night.

What are river / lake cams useful for?

Beyond watching for its own sake, fixed public cameras of this kind are widely used as reference and test footage — checking how detection or tracking models behave on real scenes instead of curated datasets.

Is it free to watch? Do I need an account?

Yes, and no account is needed. Every feed is a public YouTube stream embedded from its original channel — Camlisted links to sources and never hosts or downloads video.
