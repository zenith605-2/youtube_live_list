About resort / hotel cams

Resort cams are marketing that happens to be genuinely pleasant to watch: pool decks, ski-lift bases, beachfront terraces maintained by hotels that want the view to look good year-round. Reliability is their quiet strength — an operator with a business reason rarely lets the camera go down.

Much of this collection comes from dedicated operators — Norway Live (11 feeds), Mammoth Mountain (9 feeds), and 엘리시안강촌 (8 feeds) — channels that keep cameras running year-round rather than posting one-off clips.

Resolution figures here are measured, not scraped from titles: Camlisted records the real playback quality when a feed is opened. 24 of these feeds have been measured so far — 2 in 4K, 17 in full HD, and 2 in HD.

Condition tags mark what a clip actually shows, so you can jump straight to a scenario: currently 3 night scenes, 2 rain scenes, and 8 snow scenes tagged here.

The longest-running live camera in this set has been broadcasting continuously since 2019 — “Inn at Bay Harbor: Little Traverse Bay”.

How many resort / hotel live cams are there?

95 live resort / hotel cameras are streaming right now, plus 14 recorded clips, with the deepest coverage in United States, Japan, Norway, and Mexico. The list is re-verified every night.

What are resort / hotel cams useful for?

Beyond watching for its own sake, fixed public cameras of this kind are widely used as reference and test footage — checking how detection or tracking models behave on real scenes instead of curated datasets.
