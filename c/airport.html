About airport cams

Airport cams serve one of YouTube's great niche audiences: planespotters. Runway views with ATC audio, taxiway pans, holds short of the threshold — regulars identify every type on sight and the chat reads like a logbook. Movement is scheduled, which makes these feeds unusually predictable to test against.

Much of this collection comes from dedicated operators — Golf Oscar Romeo (7 feeds), 朝日新聞LIVE (6 feeds), and AVIAÇÃO TV (5 feeds) — channels that keep cameras running year-round rather than posting one-off clips.

Resolution figures here are measured, not scraped from titles: Camlisted records the real playback quality when a feed is opened. 19 of these feeds have been measured so far — 4 in 4K, 12 in full HD, and 2 in HD.

Condition tags mark what a clip actually shows, so you can jump straight to a scenario: currently 4 night scenes and 2 rain scenes tagged here.

The longest-running live camera in this set has been broadcasting continuously since 2020 — “いわて花巻空港ライブ映像”.

Frequently asked questions

How many airport live cams are there?

88 live airport cameras are streaming right now, plus 52 recorded clips, with the deepest coverage in United States, Japan, Portugal, and Brazil. The list is re-verified every night.

What are airport cams useful for?

Beyond watching for its own sake, fixed public cameras of this kind are widely used as reference and test footage — checking how detection or tracking models behave on real scenes instead of curated datasets.

Is it free to watch? Do I need an account?

Yes, and no account is needed. Every feed is a public YouTube stream embedded from its original channel — Camlisted links to sources and never hosts or downloads video.
