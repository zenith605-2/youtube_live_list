About parking cams

Parking lots and garages are where the classic fixed-CCTV look lives on: high angles, wide lenses, constant low-level motion. That makes them oddly watchable — and genuinely useful. Vehicle-detection and occupancy-counting models are usually trained and tested on exactly this perspective, so a steady public parking feed is one of the few places to see that footage without owning a camera.

Much of this collection comes from dedicated operators — DOMAR CCTV (13 feeds), PVS Security Integrators (4 feeds), and 草津町公式YouTubeチャンネル (4 feeds) — channels that keep cameras running year-round rather than posting one-off clips.

Resolution figures here are measured, not scraped from titles: Camlisted records the real playback quality when a feed is opened. 25 of these feeds have been measured so far — 3 in 4K, 2 in QHD, 13 in full HD, and 6 in HD.

Condition tags mark what a clip actually shows, so you can jump straight to a scenario: currently 13 night scenes and 1 rain scene tagged here.

Frequently asked questions

How many parking live cams are there?

36 live parking cameras are streaming right now, plus 59 recorded clips, with the deepest coverage in Japan, United States, United Kingdom, and South Korea. The list is re-verified every night.

What are parking cams useful for?

Beyond watching for its own sake, fixed public cameras of this kind are widely used as reference and test footage — checking how detection or tracking models behave on real scenes instead of curated datasets.

Is it free to watch? Do I need an account?

Yes, and no account is needed. Every feed is a public YouTube stream embedded from its original channel — Camlisted links to sources and never hosts or downloads video.
